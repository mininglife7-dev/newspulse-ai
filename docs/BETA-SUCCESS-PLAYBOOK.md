## Quick Start: First Day

**Goal: Complete your first successful news search within 5 minutes**

### The 5-Minute Success Path

1. **Sign Up** (1 minute)
   - Go to: https://newspulse-ai.vercel.app
   - Click "Sign Up"
   - Enter your work email
   - Check email for verification link (check spam folder!)
   - Click the link

2. **Create Workspace** (1 minute)
   - Enter your company name
   - Select your industry
   - Click "Create Workspace"
   - You should see your workspace name appear

3. **Run Your First Search** (2 minutes)
   - Type a search topic (e.g., "AI in healthcare")
   - Click "Search"
   - Wait 30-60 seconds
   - See results with AI summaries

4. **Verify Success** (1 minute)
   - You should see 10-50 articles listed
   - Each shows: Title, Source, Date, Summary
   - Click one to read full article
   - Click "View History" to see past searches

**Success Criteria:**
- ✓ You signed up successfully
- ✓ You received verification email
- ✓ You created a workspace
- ✓ You completed at least one search
- ✓ You saw results with summaries

**If you get stuck:** Email beta-support@newspulse-ai.com with what went wrong

## Common Success Patterns

### Pattern 1: Market Research

**Goal:** Stay updated on market trends and competitor activity

**Best Practices:**
1. **Create recurring search topics** (run weekly or daily)
   - "AI market trends 2026"
   - "CompetitorName news"
   - "[Your Industry] innovation"

2. **Save search history**
   - All searches automatically saved
   - Access "View History" anytime
   - Export results to spreadsheet

3. **Share findings with team**
   - Copy/paste summaries into email
   - Screenshot results
   - Forward to Slack channel

**Example Workflow:**
```
Monday morning routine:
1. Search: "Artificial Intelligence adoption 2026"
2. Save top 3 findings
3. Forward to team email list
4. Discuss findings in team meeting
```

**Metrics to Track:**
- Searches per week: Target 3-5
- Articles read per search: Target 5-10
- Time saved vs manual research: 30-60 min per week
- Decisions influenced by findings: Track monthly

### Pattern 2: Competitive Intelligence

**Goal:** Monitor what competitors are doing, funding, partnerships

**Best Practices:**
1. **Set up competitor search** (check weekly)
   - "Competitor A funding"
   - "Competitor A partnership"
   - "Competitor B product launch"
   - "Competitor C executives"

2. **Identify patterns**
   - Are they expanding? (Market entry announcements)
   - Are they hiring? (Hiring announcements)
   - Are they investing in R&D? (Acquisition activity)

3. **Act on insights**
   - Faster than manual research
   - Spot opportunities before others
   - Plan strategic responses

**Example Workflow:**
```
Weekly Competitive Intelligence (30 min):
- Search each key competitor (5 min each)
- Review summaries (3 min per competitor)
- Identify 2-3 key insights
- Share with leadership team
```

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Competitor entering your market segment
- Large funding rounds (indicates growth plans)
- Executive hires in your space (indicates strategic shift)
- Major partnerships (indicates competitive threat or alliance)

## Team Collaboration

### Adding Team Members

**Roles Available:**
- **Owner:** Can search, manage team, delete workspace
- **Editor:** Can search, view history, no admin access
- **Viewer:** View-only access to search results

**Recommended Setup:**
- 1 Owner (you)
- 2-3 Editors (daily users)
- 5-10 Viewers (stakeholders who review results)

### Collaboration Workflows

**Workflow 1: Research Team**
```
Lead Researcher (Owner/Editor):
- Runs searches on assigned topics
- Marks important findings
- Shares key articles with team

Team Member (Viewer):
- Reviews saved searches
- Reads summaries and full articles
- Provides feedback to lead researcher
```

**Workflow 2: Executive Brief**
```
Research Team (Editor):
- Runs 5-10 searches on key topics
- Saves results in workspace history

Executive (Viewer):
- Logs in Friday morning
- Reviews all weekly searches
- Gets briefed on key findings
```

**Workflow 3: Distributed Team**
```
Regional Markets (multiple Editors):
- Each covers their market region
- Searches local market news
- Shares findings in workspace

Headquarters (Owner/Viewer):
- Reviews all regional findings
- Synthesizes into global view
- Makes strategic decisions
```

## Advanced Tips & Tricks

### Tip 1: Search Formulation

**Better Searches (Will Yield Relevant Results):**
- "Artificial intelligence in healthcare 2026"
- "Machine learning regulatory compliance"
- "Electric vehicle adoption trends"

**Broader Searches (Will Yield More Results):**
- "AI trends"
- "Technology innovation"
- "Market analysis"

**More Specific Searches (Will Yield Fewer But Relevant Results):**
- "FDA approval process AI diagnostics"
- "EU AI Act compliance requirements"
- "Tesla autonomous driving regulations"

**Formula for Effective Searches:**
```
[Topic] + [Context] + [Timeframe]
Examples:
- "AI adoption" + "healthcare" + "2026"
- "Market trends" + "electric vehicles" + "next 12 months"
- "Regulation" + "data privacy" + "EU"
```

### Tip 2: Search Cadence

**Daily Searches:** For fast-moving topics
- Breaking news in your industry
- Stock market/financial impacts
- Security threats
- Executive/M&A activity

**Weekly Searches:** For strategic trends
- Market analysis
- Competitor activity
- Regulatory changes
- Industry trends

**Monthly Searches:** For long-term perspectives
- Market size & growth
- Technology maturity curves
- Industry consolidation
- 5-10 year forecasts

### Tip 3: Information Synthesis

**Beyond Individual Searches:**
1. Run multiple related searches (5 on same topic)
2. Save all results
3. Compare findings across searches
4. Identify consensus (what most sources agree on)
5. Identify disagreements (areas of debate)
6. Form your own conclusions

**Example:**
```
Topic: "Future of AI in Customer Service"
Search 5 variations:
1. AI customer service trends
2. Chatbot adoption rates
3. Customer sentiment on AI support
4. Companies investing in AI support
5. AI customer service ROI

Compare: What's the consensus? What's debated?
Synthesize: What's our take?
```

### Tip 4: Exporting Results

**Current Export Options:**
- Copy/paste summaries into docs
- Take screenshots for sharing
- Email links to colleagues
- Export to note-taking app (Notion, OneNote)

**Recommended Tools:**
- **For Writing:** Copy results into Google Docs
- **For Presentations:** Screenshot results into PowerPoint
- **For Knowledge Base:** Save to Notion or Obsidian
- **For Email:** Forward results to team

## Avoiding Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: One-Time Use

**Problem:** "I signed up, did one search, and haven't come back"

**Why It Happens:** 
- Didn't see the value in recurring searches
- Forgot about the tool
- Didn't integrate into workflow

**Prevention:**
- Schedule recurring searches (same time each week)
- Share results with team (keeps it visible)
- Use results to inform decisions (shows value)

**Fix:** Start with 1 recurring search topic, run every Friday morning

---

### Mistake 2: Unactionable Searches

**Problem:** "I searched but the results didn't help me"

**Why It Happens:**
- Search was too broad ("technology")
- Search was too narrow ("Article from May 3rd 2025")
- Search didn't match your actual need

**Prevention:**
- Think about what you'll DO with results before searching
- Use specific keywords (see Tip 1)
- Test search phrasing

**Fix:** Reframe search to match your actual use case
```
Bad: "AI"
Good: "AI in healthcare industry"

Bad: "Market"
Good: "Electric vehicle market size growth 2026"
```

---

### Mistake 3: Not Sharing Results

**Problem:** "I find great stuff but the team doesn't know"

**Why It Happens:**
- Results only in your account
- No easy way to share
- Team doesn't know you're using the tool

**Prevention:**
- Invite team members to your workspace
- Share search results via email
- Mention in team meetings

**Fix:** Send weekly email to team:
```
Subject: This Week's Industry Insights

Here are this week's top findings from NewsPulse AI:

1. [Finding] — [Source]
   Implication: [Why it matters]

2. [Finding] — [Source]
   Implication: [Why it matters]

Read full summaries in NewsPulse (workspace link)
```

---

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Search Quality

**Problem:** "I got results but they don't seem relevant"

**Why It Happens:**
- AI summaries sometimes miss context
- Results from all dates (not just recent)
- Article titles can be misleading

**Prevention:**
- Click through to read full articles (don't just read summaries)
- Check publication date
- Verify source credibility
- Read 3-5 articles before forming conclusions

**Fix:** Invest 5 minutes reading full articles, not just summaries

## Getting Unstuck

### Support Resources

**In Order of Response Time:**

1. **Self-Help (Instant)**
   - Read: BETA-CUSTOMER-ONBOARDING.md → Troubleshooting section
   - Check: Search FAQ for your issue

2. **Email Support (2-4 hours)**
   - beta-support@newspulse-ai.com
   - Include: Error message, what you were trying, screenshots

3. **Urgent Issues (1 hour)**
   - Use subject line: "[URGENT]" for faster response
   - We'll prioritize urgent requests

4. **Direct Escalation (Immediate, for critical issues)**
   - If service is down or you can't use product at all
   - Reply to support email with "[CRITICAL]" prefix
   - We'll page the on-call engineer

### Common Questions

**Q: How often should I search?**
A: Depends on your use case. Market research: 1-2/week. Competitive intelligence: 2-3/week. Risk monitoring: 1/month. Start with 1 search/week and increase as you find value.

**Q: Can I export results?**
A: Currently: copy/paste or screenshot. CSV export coming in General Availability.

**Q: How long do searches take?**
A: Typically 30-60 seconds. Sometimes longer if external services are slow.

**Q: Can I see what articles were found but not summarized?**
A: No, we only return articles with AI summaries. This helps quality.

**Q: Can my team collaborate?**
A: Yes! Invite team members as Owner/Editor/Viewer. All searches appear in shared history.

**Q: Is my data private?**
A: Yes. We use Row-Level Security so users only see their own workspace.

**Q: Can I delete my account?**
A: Yes. Go to Settings → Account → Delete Account. All your data is deleted.
